# VIVIM Product Roadmap

## Product Vision

VIVIM becomes the **essential infrastructure** for every human interacting with AI—the sovereign layer that sits between you and every AI system, preserving your memory, protecting your rights, and enabling you to capture value from your intellectual history.

---

## Product Principles

1. **Local-first**: Your data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose
2. **Encryption by default**: Keys exist only in your memory or hardware
3. **Granular consent**: You decide what stays private, what shares, what sells
4. **Measurable privacy**: You can see and verify where your data went
5. **Interoperable**: Works across all AI providers, present and future

---

## Product Releases

### v1.0: The Sovereign Vault (Launch)

**Target**: Month 9

**What's Built**:
- Unified vault for all AI conversations
- Import from ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Cursor
- Browser extension for capture
- Local semantic search
- Encrypted storage with key hierarchy

**User Experience**:
> "I import my ChatGPT history and suddenly I can search across every AI conversation I've ever had. They're all in one place, encrypted, mine."

**Who It's For**:
- Heavy AI users who want unified memory
- Privacy-conscious individuals
- People leaving one AI for another

**Pricing Model**: Free for personal use (v1.0)

---

### v2.0: The Rights Layer (Launch + 3 months)

**Target**: Month 12

**What's Built**:
- Automatic ownership classification
- Personal vs. work vs. regulated tagging
- Workspace modes (personal/work)
- Co-governance basics (TDASS v1)
- Basic detection (canary tokens)

**User Experience**:
> "My vault automatically separates my personal conversations from work discussions. When I try to export company-confidential material, it warns me and shows what approvals are needed."

**Who It's For**:
- Professionals using AI at work
- Anyone with sensitive conversations
- Enterprises wanting employee-friendly privacy

**Pricing Model**: Free for personal use, enterprise licensing for co-governance

---

### v3.0: The Marketplace (Launch + 6 months)

**Target**: Month 15

**What's Built**:
- Data marketplace infrastructure
- Listing with ZK proofs
- Purchase and delivery flow
- Revenue splitting for co-governed data
- Basic reputation system

**User Experience**:
> "I can sell specific conversations about my expertise in technical writing. A research company buys them to improve their model. I get paid, my co-governed work data splits revenue with my company. Everyone consents."

**Who It's For**:
- Domain experts with valuable data
- Researchers needing training data
- AI companies needing quality data

**Pricing Model**: Transaction fee (15%) on sales

---

### v4.0: The Sentinel (Launch + 9 months)

**Target**: Month 18

**What's Built**:
- Full detection suite (13 algorithms)
- Evidence package generation
- Cross-provider leak detection
- Opt-out verification
- Collective Sovereignty Index

**User Experience**:
> "The Sentinel shows me that GPT-5 likely trained on 312 of my conversations despite my opt-out. It generates a cryptographic evidence package with timestamps and proofs. I can send this to a lawyer."

**Who It's For**:
- Legal teams building cases
- Privacy advocates
- Anyone who opted out and wants verification
- Journalists investigating AI training

**Pricing Model**: Subscription ($9.99/month) for detection, free evidence generation

---

### v5.0: The Network (Launch + 12 months)

**Target**: Month 21

**What's Built**:
- Multi-device sync (P2P)
- Anonymous collective detection
- Shared memory circles
- Advanced MCP context
- Mobile full feature

**User Experience**:
> "My phone and laptop sync seamlessly. I join a collective detection with 10,000 other users—we anonymously verify which providers respect opt-outs. The Sovereignty Index shows who to trust."

**Who It's For**:
- Multi-device users
- Privacy communities
- Research collectives

**Pricing Model**: Free for basic sync, premium for collective features
